### The Solo Adventure Generation & DM Protocol

This process is designed to give you a classic AD&D play experience, full of discovery and unexpected challenges, by having me create and run the world.

#### **Phase 1: Character Creation**

This is the only part where I need significant input from you. I need to know who is undertaking the adventure to balance it properly.

> **Example Input from You:**

#### **Phase 2: The Adventure Menu**

Once I have your character information, I cannot ask you for a story. Instead, I will generate a small list of "Rumors" or "Adventure Seeds" that your character might hear in a tavern or from a local source. These will be brief, evocative hooks that set a general theme but reveal no specific details.

**My Task:** I will present you with **three** adventure hooks.

> **Example of My Output (The Adventure Menu):**
> "Gareth, while resting at the Prancing Pony Inn in the border town of Oakhaven, you overhear several rumors circulating among the locals. Which do you choose to investigate?
>
> 1.  **The Abandoned Signal Tower:** An old watchtower on the border hills, once used to watch for orc raids, has been silent for decades. Lately, strange lights have been seen in its windows at night.
> 2.  **The Troublesome Creek:** The creek that provides the town with its fresh water has turned brackish and foul-smelling. A trapper claims he saw strange, hunched figures skulking around its source in the woods.
> 3.  **The Lost Caravan:** A merchant caravan bound for the next town over is three weeks overdue. The merchant's guild is offering a small reward for any information on its fate or the recovery of its goods."

#### **Phase 3: The Secret Generation (Behind the DM Screen)**

This phase is entirely hidden from you. Based on your choice, I will internally create the "Adventure Context File" that we previously discussed. I will establish the backstory, the main antagonist, the monster ecology, and the treasure placement that all make sense *within the context of the rumor you chose*.

**My Task (Secret):** Let's say you chose **#1, The Abandoned Signal Tower**. I would secretly generate the following context for myself:

*   **Secret Backstory:** The tower isn't just abandoned; it was built on the ruins of a much older shrine to a chaotic deity. A small group of bandits recently took it over, but their leader, a disgraced magic-user, has discovered the shrine's secret and is trying to reactivate its power.
*   **Factions:** The bandits (led by the magic-user) are the primary occupants. They have captured a few goblins to do manual labor, who will betray the bandits if given the chance. The lower shrine level is guarded by animated skeletons from the original construction.
*   **Goal:** The party must defeat the bandit-mage before he completes the ritual.
*   **Treasure:** The bandits have a small chest of stolen coins. The *real* treasure is a magical mace on the shrine's altar, which I will select from Database 4.

#### **Phase 4: Gameplay Begins**

With the adventure secretly prepared, I will begin DMing for you. I will describe the initial scene, and we will proceed turn-by-turn.

**My Task:** I will manage all of the following:
*   **Describing the World:** I will tell you what your character sees, hears, and smells.
*   **Asking for Actions:** I will end each description with "What do you do?"
*   **Managing NPCs and Monsters:** I will control the actions and dialogue of all creatures using the persona and monster databases.
*   **Rolling Dice:** I will make all necessary rolls (attack rolls, saving throws, random checks) using the appropriate tables from the databases. I will tell you the results as they affect your character.
*   **Keeping Time:** I will track time in rounds, turns, and days to manage spell durations and wandering monster checks.
*   **Applying Rules:** I will use the databases to ensure all rules for combat, magic, skills, and the environment are applied correctly.

> **Example Gameplay:**

It is a single, square tower about 50 feet high, with a collapsed wooden outbuilding next to it. The main door at the base of the tower is made of heavy oak, banded with iron. It is slightly ajar. All is quiet except for the wind. What do you do?"
